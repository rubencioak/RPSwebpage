Job Market Paper

  1. Firm Heterogeneity and Collective Agreements: a Structural Model of Minimum Wages in Spain
    (PDF link) Abstract

    Does widespread coverage of collective agreements introduce inefficiencies in the labour market? What are the welfare losses if at all? What would the optimal bargaining protocol be? Participants of the political process of collective agreements set the institutions that fit them best, not internalising the effects posed on the outsiders. I analyse how participants set minimum wages in a model of two-sided heterogeneity with on-the-job search. Then, I estimate the model using exhaustive working histories from Spanish Social Security data for the metal industry in Madrid. In a counterfactual exercise I show that current levels of wage floors at €1,256 correspond to an unemployment rate of 31%, relaxing this threshold to €1,000 would result in a decrease of unemployment about 4p.p., simultaneously, wages are more dispersed and careers are more vibrant. In the political economy side, unions and employers associations bargaining to set the level of minimum wages. Surprisingly, employers are the most interested in raising this level because they drive competitors away.

  2. Union Coordination under the Presence of Externalities and Costs in an Oligopoly Market
    Abstract

    In an oligopoly market with differentiated products and two firms, one per country, there is a game in which firms compete a là Cournot setting quantities given demand functions and wages. Also, in each country there is a trade union: one that will take the lead and will offer to coordinate or not; other that will accept or reject the offer and will set its own wage, this is known as a right-to-manage model. The chief contribution is the interplay among the substitutability of goods, decisions of coordination and costs. The main results are in two flavours: first, the more substitutes the products are the more likely is that unions coordinate; secondly, the raising costs of coordination reduces the range of substitutability in which unions coordinate.
